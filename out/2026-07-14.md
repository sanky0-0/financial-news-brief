### Rates & Central Banks
**Angola: Treasury foreign exchange sales jump to 1.626 billion dollars in the first half of 2026** — [→ financialafrik.com](https://www.financialafrik.com/2026/07/14/angola-les-ventes-de-devises-du-tresor-bondissent-a-1626-milliard-de-dollars-au-premier-semestre-2026/)

**Why this matters:**
- Angola’s $1.626B in H1 2026 FX sales signals a deliberate central bank liquidity push, likely aimed at stabilizing the kwanza (AOA) and curbing import-driven inflation above 25% — critical for Angolan Eurobond holders and local banks like Banco Angolano de Investimentos.
- The jump in dollar supply directly benefits Angolan importers in food and energy, notably Sonangol, by reducing parallel market premium and lowering their real debt-service costs on USD-denominated liabilities.
- For frontier market debt investors, sustained FX sales at this pace could improve Angola’s ability to meet its $3.5B in external debt repayments due in 2026, tightening spreads on the ANGOL 9.375% 2027s.
- This move places pressure on the Banco Nacional de Angola to maintain high reserve coverage ratios, with any depletion below 6 months of imports risking a reversal of recent kwanza gains and hitting Portuguese banks with Angolan exposure, such as Banco BPI.
